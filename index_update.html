Biography

About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at the Unmanned Systems Technology Research Institute, Northwestern Polytechnical University (NWPU). My work aims to build visual intelligence systems that are both robust in real-world environments and efficient in terms of data and supervision.

My research has two closely connected directions. One is robust visual perception, especially for degraded inputs, non-salient targets, and remote sensing imagery. The other is multimodal modeling, where I explore multimodal large language models and vision-language agents for image understanding, generation, editing, and tool use.

Papers from these efforts have appeared in venues including CVPR, ECCV, IJCAI, IEEE Transactions on Image Processing, IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems, and IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video Technology.

I am particularly interested in bridging robust perception and multimodal reasoning, so that models can not only see better in difficult conditions, but also act more intelligently with visual inputs.
